This is actually an excellent topic for 8–15 year olds because they use YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, and WhatsApp every day.

Misinformation & Fake News

Start With a Question

Ask:

> If someone posts on TikTok that schools will be closed for 1 month, would you immediately believe it?



Some students will say yes.

Then explain:

> Not everything on the internet is true.




---

What is Misinformation?

Simple definition:

> Misinformation is false information that people share, sometimes without realizing it's false.



Example:

Someone forwards a WhatsApp message:

> "Eating chocolate increases your height by 10 cm."



People share it, but it's not true.


---

What is Fake News?

> Fake news is made-up news that is presented as if it were real.



Example:

A thumbnail says:

> "Famous YouTuber arrested!"



But when you open the video, it's completely false.


---

Why Do People Spread Fake News?

Ask students to guess.

Possible reasons:

1. More Views

People want:

Likes

Subscribers

Money


Shocking titles get clicks.


---

2. To Trick People

Some people want:

Money

Passwords

Personal information



---

3. They Don't Know It's False

Someone receives a message and forwards it without checking.

This is very common on WhatsApp.


---

Examples Students Understand

Example 1: Gaming

"Free Robux Generator"

Ask:

> Real or Fake?



Usually fake.


---

Example 2: AI Images

Show or describe:

Flying elephants

Giant sharks in cities

Celebrities doing strange things


Explain:

> AI can create realistic images that never happened.




---

Example 3: YouTube Titles

Examples:

"Minecraft is being deleted tomorrow!"

"ChatGPT has been banned forever!"


Ask:

> How can we know if it's true?




---

How to Detect Fake News

Teach the "STOP" Method.

S – Stop

Don't immediately believe or share.


---

T – Think

Ask:

> Does this sound realistic?




---

O – Observe

Look at:

Who posted it?

Is it a trusted source?

Is there evidence?



---

P – Prove

Check another source.

If only one random account says it, be careful.


---

Activity: True or Suspicious?

Read statements.

Students vote.

Statement 1

"The sun rises in the east."

✅ True


---

Statement 2

"Drinking water makes you invisible."

❌ Fake


---

Statement 3

"Some photos online are created by AI."

✅ True


---

Statement 4

"Click here to get unlimited free money."

❌ Fake


---

Connect It To AI

Ask:

> Can AI create fake pictures?



Yes.

> Can AI write fake stories?



Yes.

> Can AI make fake voices?



Yes.

Then explain:

> This is why we must think carefully before believing things online.




---

Key Lesson to Write on the Board

Before You Believe Something Online:

1. Who posted it?


2. Is there evidence?


3. Can I verify it somewhere else?


4. Does it sound realistic?


5. Should I share it immediately?



If students remember these five questions, they will be far more resistant to fake news and misinformation than most adults.
