For children aged 6–11, Internet Safety should be practical, not technical. Focus on habits they can actually remember.

Internet Safety for Kids

Rule 1: Keep Personal Information Private

Never share:

Full home address

Phone number

Passwords

School name

Parents' information


Ask:

> If a stranger on the internet asks your address, should you tell them?



Answer: ❌ No


---

Rule 2: Strong Passwords

Explain:

Bad passwords:

123456

password

abc123


Better passwords:

TigerBlue99

MangoRocket45


You don't need to teach complex cybersecurity.

Just tell them:

> Never share your password, even with friends.




---

Rule 3: Strangers Online Are Still Strangers

Ask:

> If a stranger comes to your house and asks to enter, would you allow them?



Usually they say no.

Then explain:

> The same rule applies online.



If someone they don't know messages them:

Don't share information

Tell parents or teachers



---

Rule 4: Think Before You Click

Show examples:

"You won $1,000,000!"

"Click here to get free Robux!"

"Free iPhone!"


Explain:

> If something sounds too good to be true, it probably isn't true.




---

Rule 5: Be Kind Online

Examples:

✅ Good:

Helping others

Encouraging friends


❌ Bad:

Insulting

Bullying

Mean comments


Explain:

> Treat people online the same way you would treat them in real life.




---

Rule 6: Ask an Adult If Unsure

If they see:

Scary content

Strange messages

Something confusing


They should tell:

Parents

Teacher

Trusted adult



---

Rule 7: Not Everything Online Is True

Example:

Ask:

> If someone says "Chocolate grows on trees," should we believe it immediately?



No.

Explain:

> Sometimes people post wrong information online.




---

Fun Activity (10 Minutes)

Ask students:

Safe or Not Safe?

1. Sharing your password with a friend ❌


2. Asking parents before downloading a game ✅


3. Talking to a stranger online ❌


4. Being kind in comments ✅


5. Clicking "Free Robux" ads ❌


6. Telling a teacher about a scary message ✅



Let students answer by raising hands.


---

The 5 Golden Rules

Write these on the board:

🛡️ Keep personal information private.

🛡️ Never share passwords.

🛡️ Don't talk to strangers online.

🛡️ Think before you click.

🛡️ Tell a trusted adult if something feels wrong.

These five rules are enough for a memorable and useful Internet Safety lesson for your Little Genius students.

An 8–15 age range is much broader, and the older students (12–15) will get bored if the class feels too childish. You should aim your teaching at roughly the 11–13 year old level and simplify only when needed for younger students.

For Internet Safety, I'd teach it like this:

Internet Safety (8–15 Years)

1. Why Internet Safety Matters

Ask:

> Would you leave your house door unlocked?



Most will say no.

Then explain:

> Your online accounts are like your digital house. They also need protection.




---

2. Password Security

Ask:

> Which password is stronger?



123456

shehzad123

TigerRocket#89


Discuss why.

Teach:

Use long passwords.

Don't reuse the same password everywhere.

Never share passwords.



---

3. Scams & Fake Messages

Examples:

Free Robux

Free UC

Free Diamonds

Free Mobile Legends Skins

"Congratulations! You won a phone."


Ask:

> Who has seen messages like these?



Explain that scammers often target gamers.


---

4. Social Media Safety

Discuss:

Don't share private information publicly.

Be careful about posting photos.

Not everyone online is who they claim to be.


Ask:

> Would you meet a stranger from the internet alone?




---

5. Cyberbullying

Explain:

Mean comments

Fake rumors

Harassment in games


Ask:

> What should someone do if they are being bullied online?



Good answers:

Block

Report

Tell parents/teachers



---

6. AI Safety

This fits your course perfectly.

Ask:

> Is everything ChatGPT says always correct?



Explain:

AI can make mistakes.

Always verify important information.

Don't share personal information with AI tools.



---

7. Activity: Safe or Unsafe?

Give scenarios.

Scenario 1

A stranger asks for your phone number in a game chat.

Safe or Unsafe?

❌ Unsafe

Scenario 2

You ask your parents before downloading an app.

✅ Safe

Scenario 3

You use the same password everywhere.

❌ Unsafe

Scenario 4

You report a suspicious message.

✅ Safe
